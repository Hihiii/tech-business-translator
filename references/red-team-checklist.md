# Red-team Checklist

Use before finalizing high-risk business communication.

## Factual Integrity

- Are all numbers sourced from the input or clearly labeled as estimates?
- Are unknowns explicitly named?
- Are confirmed facts separated from assumptions?
- Did the output preserve all material constraints?

## Risk Transparency

- Did the output hide, soften, or exaggerate risk?
- Does the audience understand what could still go wrong?
- Are residual risks and monitoring plans visible?

## Legal and Customer Safety

- Does the output avoid unapproved admission of fault?
- Does it avoid saying "breach" unless confirmed?
- Does it avoid promising permanent prevention?
- Does it state whether customer action is required?

## Information Disclosure

- Does the output expose internal system names unnecessarily?
- Does it reveal sensitive controls, credentials, logs, or exploit details?
- Does it include internal-only metrics in external communication?

## Bilingual Consistency

- Do English and Traditional Chinese versions carry the same severity?
- Are numbers, dates, and action items identical?
- Did the Chinese version soften legal or security risk?
- Did either language add claims absent from the other?
