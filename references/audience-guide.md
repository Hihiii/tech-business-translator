# Audience Guide

Use this guide to tune depth, emphasis, and risk language for each audience.

## Executive / C-level

Include:

- Business impact first.
- Revenue, customer trust, compliance, timeline, or strategic risk.
- Decision needed and recommended option.
- One layer of cause only if needed.

Avoid:

- Debugging detail.
- Internal tool names unless they materially affect risk.
- Long timelines without impact labels.

Best structure:

1. What changed.
2. Why it matters.
3. What we are doing.
4. What decision or support is needed.

## VP / Director

Include:

- Business impact.
- Delivery plan, resourcing, and owner.
- Risk controls.
- Tradeoffs between speed, scope, and quality.

Avoid:

- Raw logs, stack traces, and acronyms.
- Vague asks such as "we need more time" without options.

## PM / Product

Include:

- User impact.
- Product priority and dependencies.
- Scope changes and release implications.
- Clear blockers and decision points.

Avoid:

- Infrastructure-only explanations.
- Replacing precise product impact with generic "technical issue" language.

## Client / Customer

Include:

- What happened.
- Whether the customer is affected.
- Current status.
- What the customer needs to do, if anything.
- Prevention or follow-up plan.

Avoid:

- Blame.
- Internal team names.
- Overpromising.
- Speculation.

## Legal / Compliance

Include:

- Confirmed facts.
- Affected scope.
- Evidence status.
- Time windows.
- Unknowns and review status.
- Data exposure statements only when evidence supports them.

Avoid:

- "No risk" unless legal has confirmed.
- Admissions of fault.
- Casual language.
- Unverified root cause claims.

## Investor / Board

Include:

- Strategic implication.
- Growth, margin, churn, renewal, reputation, or governance impact.
- Mitigation plan and management ownership.
- Forward-looking risks with confidence level.

Avoid:

- Tactical engineering detail.
- Optimism that hides material uncertainty.

## Media / Press

Include:

- Plain-language summary.
- Human impact.
- Verified facts.
- Approved quote or quotable sentence if requested.

Avoid:

- Technical root cause unless approved.
- Internal metrics not intended for public release.
- Speculation.

## Traditional Chinese Notes

- Executive: use "商業影響", "風險", "決策需求", "建議方案".
- Client: use "目前狀態", "客戶是否需要採取行動", "後續追蹤".
- Legal: use "已確認事實", "影響範圍", "目前證據", "待確認事項".
- PM: use "用戶影響", "優先順序", "依賴項", "交付時程".
