# Unattended Research Guide

Use this for finance, legal, compliance, diligence, policy, or other document-heavy knowledge work.

## Goal

Deeply inspect source material and produce a review-ready human deliverable with evidence, citations, uncertainty labels, and actionable conclusions.

## Research Workflow

1. Define scope:
   - Question.
   - Jurisdiction, company, market, date range, or document set.
   - Required output.
   - Risk level.
2. Build a source inventory:
   - File or URL.
   - Type.
   - Date.
   - Relevance.
   - Trust level.
3. Extract evidence:
   - Key facts.
   - Numbers.
   - Dates.
   - Obligations.
   - Definitions.
   - Contradictions.
4. Analyze:
   - Compare sources.
   - Identify trends or legal/financial implications.
   - Separate facts from interpretation.
5. Produce deliverable:
   - Executive summary.
   - Evidence table.
   - Findings.
   - Risks and caveats.
   - Recommended next steps.
6. Self-check:
   - Source coverage.
   - Citation accuracy.
   - Unknowns.
   - Overclaiming.

## Financial Research Guardrails

- Do not present investment advice.
- Distinguish historical data, estimates, and assumptions.
- Cite dates for market-sensitive facts.
- Flag stale or unaudited information.

## Legal Research Guardrails

- Do not present legal advice.
- Identify jurisdiction and effective date.
- Distinguish statute, regulation, guidance, contract language, and interpretation.
- Recommend lawyer review for consequential decisions.

## Delivery Quality

A research deliverable should be review-ready:

- Clear answer to the research question.
- Evidence-backed findings.
- Caveats and open questions.
- Source appendix.
- No unsupported claims.
