# Tone Guide

Use this guide to select safe, audience-appropriate wording.

## Formal

Use for reports, executive updates, official emails, and board materials.

Pattern:

- State the status.
- Explain the impact.
- Describe action and timeline.
- Close with decision or next step.

Avoid casual phrasing, jokes, and unsupported certainty.

## Urgent

Use for active incidents, high-priority risks, and time-sensitive decisions.

Pattern:

- "Current status: ..."
- "Business impact: ..."
- "Immediate action: ..."
- "Next update: ..."

Avoid long background. Do not bury the next action.

## Diplomatic

Use for delays, setbacks, missed commitments, or customer-facing bad news.

Pattern:

- Acknowledge the impact.
- State ownership without blaming individuals.
- Explain the mitigation.
- Clarify what will change.

Useful English:

- "We identified a gap in..."
- "We are taking the following corrective actions..."
- "The current evidence indicates..."

Useful Traditional Chinese:

- "我們已確認此問題造成..."
- "團隊正在採取以下修正行動..."
- "依目前證據判斷..."

## Client-safe

Use for external customer communication.

Rules:

- Separate confirmed facts from investigation.
- Say whether customer action is required.
- Avoid internal architecture names unless necessary.
- Avoid blame and speculation.

## Legal-safe

Use for security, compliance, contract, or liability-sensitive updates.

Rules:

- Use "current evidence" and "currently known" when facts may change.
- Do not say "no impact" unless verified.
- Do not say "breach" unless legally confirmed.
- Include "under review" when appropriate.

## Board-ready

Use for board and governance audiences.

Rules:

- Connect issue to strategy, governance, risk, and accountability.
- Include management action and owner.
- Include residual risk.
- Avoid low-level operational detail.

## Investor-ready

Use for investor updates.

Rules:

- Tie work to growth, retention, margin, or market position.
- Be optimistic only when evidence supports it.
- Make execution risks visible.

## Crisis Communication

Use during high-impact incidents or public-sensitive events.

Rules:

- Lead with safety, customer impact, and current status.
- Update at a predictable cadence.
- Keep language factual and calm.
- Do not provide premature root cause.
