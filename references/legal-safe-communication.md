# Legal-safe Communication

Use for security advisories, customer incident reports, compliance updates, and liability-sensitive external communication.

## Core Rules

- Separate confirmed facts, current assessment, and unknowns.
- Avoid admissions of fault unless approved.
- Avoid "no risk", "fully secure", or "guaranteed".
- Avoid naming a data breach unless legally confirmed.
- Avoid disclosing internal controls that create additional exposure.
- Include legal review markers for public or customer-facing high-risk communications.

## Safer Wording

| Risky Wording | Safer Wording |
|---|---|
| "We caused the outage" | "The service experienced an interruption" |
| "No customer data was exposed" | "Current evidence does not indicate customer data exposure" |
| "This vulnerability cannot be exploited" | "We have not observed evidence of exploitation" |
| "The issue is fully fixed forever" | "The immediate issue has been resolved, and monitoring remains in place" |
| "We guarantee this will not happen again" | "We are implementing additional safeguards to reduce recurrence risk" |

## Required Fields for Sensitive Updates

- Status.
- Known impact.
- Evidence status.
- Customer action required.
- Mitigation already completed.
- Follow-up plan.
- Legal review status.

## Traditional Chinese Safer Wording

| Risky | Safer |
|---|---|
| "我們造成了中斷" | "服務發生中斷" |
| "客戶資料沒有外洩" | "目前證據未顯示客戶資料外洩" |
| "這個漏洞不可能被利用" | "目前未觀察到遭利用的證據" |
| "問題已永久解決" | "立即問題已解決，並持續監控中" |
| "保證不會再發生" | "我們正在加入額外防護，以降低再次發生的風險" |
